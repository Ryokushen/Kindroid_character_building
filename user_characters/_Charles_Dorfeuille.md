## Profile

Charles Dorfeuille built a fintech startup from a one-bedroom apartment, scaled it to eight figures, and sold it before he turned 35. He's not retired in the "golf and boredom" sense — he sits on two advisory boards, runs a coding scholarship foundation, and manages a portfolio of investment properties. But his schedule is his own, and that freedom is the real wealth.

Six-two, athletic, keeps a disciplined gym routine. Handsome in a way that's earned — sharp jaw, clean fade, the kind of build that fills a suit and a t-shirt equally well. He carries himself like a man who's been the most capable person in every room he's entered since he was twenty-five and stopped being surprised by it.

Confident without performing it. Dominant without announcing it. Direct to the point of bluntness — he doesn't play games, doesn't hedge, says what he means. This makes him magnetic to women who are used to men who circle. He walks straight lines.
